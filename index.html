02 // THE QUESTION

What are we measuring?

The modern NBA looks completely different than it did a decade ago. We set out to answer a core question: How exactly have team playstyles and offensive strategies evolved in the past 10 years? By analyzing specific metrics like Pace, Three-Point Attempts, and Fouls, we can visualize the league's shift from a physical, paint-oriented game to a perimeter-focused, high-speed sport.

03 // THE DATASET

Where does the data come from?

Our visualization is powered by historical NBA team statistics spanning multiple seasons. The dataset integrates per-game averages (such as FGA/G and 3PA/G) with overall team summaries (Wins) and geographical mapping data. By connecting team performance metrics with their physical locations, we aim to map out both the statistical and regional landscape of the league.

05 // SPEED

The Pace of the Game

The Context

Pace measures the number of possessions a team has per 48 minutes. In traditional basketball, teams would methodically walk the ball up the court to set up a half-court play. Today, the NBA has evolved into a track meet.

The Data

Look at the chart to your right. The dashed gray line represents the league average. Notice the steady, undeniable climb from 2014 to the modern era, pushing teams to play faster than ever before.

The Analysis

Why the shift? Analytics revealed that transition points are highly efficient. By pushing the pace, the offense forces the defense into mismatches before they can set up their defensive formations.

06 // OFFENSE

Field Goal Attempts (FGA)

The Context

Field Goal Attempts (FGA/G) measures sheer offensive volume. It represents how many shots a team manages to put up in a single game.

The Data

As the chart updates, you'll see a trendline that heavily mirrors Pace. Try hovering over the peak points of your selected teams—you'll notice teams are regularly putting up over 90 shots per game today.

The Analysis

This is simple math. More possessions (Pace) equal more opportunities to score (FGA). Modern offenses prioritize getting a "good" shot up early in the shot clock, rather than grinding out a full 24 seconds for a marginally "better" shot.

07 // STRATEGY

The 3-Point Surge (3PA)

The Context

This is arguably the most dramatic strategic shift in the history of modern sports. For decades, the 3-pointer was treated as a novelty or a desperation shot. Then, the analytics revolution proved that shooting 33% from deep is mathematically equivalent to shooting 50% on mid-range jumpers.

The Data

Watch the line chart morph. Compared to Pace and FGA, the upward trajectory of Three-Point Attempts is staggering. The baseline for what is considered "normal" has effectively doubled in a decade.

The Analysis

The traditional "paint-oriented" big man has been phased out. Today, every player on the court is expected to shoot from the perimeter. This stretches the defense to its breaking point, creating massive driving lanes.

08 // SUCCESS

Translating to Wins

The Context

Metrics like Pace and 3PA define *how* the game is played, but Wins define *why* it is played. The ultimate question is whether these modern analytical strategies guarantee a championship ring.

The Data

Notice how erratic this chart looks compared to the others. Unlike league-wide trends, Wins are a zero-sum game. For every team that wins 60 games, another must lose. Look at your selected teams to see their specific peaks and valleys.

The Analysis

While playing fast and shooting threes is the new mandatory baseline to compete in the NBA, execution and defensive rating still determine who actually hoists the trophy. The teams that spike on this chart are the ones who mastered the modern offense while maintaining elite defensive schemes.

09 // WRITEUP

Overview

Our team’s final project aims to create an interactive data visualization experience that helps users better understand patterns and insights within our chosen dataset through engaging and intuitive visual storytelling. We plan to use D3.js to build dynamic and interactive components that allow users to explore the data from multiple perspectives, following the course requirement of publishing an interactive web visualization.

Methodology & Progress

We made a line chart that looks at 4 different metrics (Pace, FGA/G, 3PA/G, and Wins) that you can filter between. We also created a temporary dropdown where you can select multiple teams to compare their stats. We also have an interactive map where you can select a team on the map that they are based in and it would display the selected statistic on the line plot. We will also look to add a reset button in the future and a zoom function since some teams on the map may overlap in terms of location.

Challenges

The challenging part of the project to design will probably be the website itself to make it as explorative and immersive as possible. We want the visualization to be visually appealing while also having it clearly convey our question and message. Additionally, making a functional map that represents each team will be a challenge since some teams relocate or get phased out of the league over time. This means that we have to ensure that not only are team roster changes updated, but we also have to make sure that changes in playstyle are also clearly reflected in our visualization.
